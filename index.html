01 The conversation

Every creative shop in Miami is having the same conversation with the same clients. The client read a post. The client saw a demo. The client wants to know why a month of content costs what it costs.

The honest answer is that the tools do not know the brand. They do not know the three things the CMO cares about, the one thing the CEO quietly hates, the legal notes from last year's launch, the reason a particular word is off-limits. That context lives in the principal's head. Every project re-creates it. Margin leaks out through the rework.

02 The frame we use
60%

Traditional workflow

Briefs, approvals, asset delivery, billing. The stuff that belongs in Postgres and a tight spreadsheet.

30%

Rule-based logic

Style guides, brand-voice rules, compliance checks, approval chains. Deterministic by design.

10%

Genuine AI work

Pattern-matching across history, drafting against a known voice, clustering themes across a client's catalog.

03 Where the leverage sits

Most shops put AI on the wrong ten percent. The real leverage is pushing the context layer down into a structure the whole team, including AI tools, can read.

We did a version of this inside KPMG UK, one of the Big Four. Forty-plus executives, methodology handed over rather than a black-box service delivered. The posture mapped. Senior audience, professional-services buyer, owning the output at the end.

The methodology is published. The paper is called Interpretable Context Methodology. It lays out how to structure client context as a layered filesystem so a junior producer or an AI tool can read the same brand voice the principal carries in their head. Read it here: github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-.
